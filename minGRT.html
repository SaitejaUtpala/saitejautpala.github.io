**Blitz Intro to group representation theory in 15 definition and 3 Theorems for Steerable CNNs** These are notes I have prepared for understanding Steerable CNNs. We will assume that $|G| < \infty$, $|V| < \infty$ and underlying field is $\mathbb{C}$, but some of results hold with weaker hypotheses like $|V|$ being countably finite and fields having characteristic of zero. Also, some of results change if the underlying field is $\mathbb{R}$ like number of equivalence classes of irreducible reps need not be equal to number conjugacy classes. Group Representations =============================================================================== Def1 : *Group Action*: Let $G$ be group and $X$ be any set. Group action is homomorphism from group to Symmetric Group on $X$ i.e., $r: G \rightarrow \text{Sym}(X)$ Def2 : *Group Representation*: G be Group, V be Vectorspace then $\rho : G \rightarrow \text{GL}(V)$ s.t $\rho$ is homomorphism. Def3 : *Matrix Representation*: G be Group then $\rho : G \rightarrow \text{GL}_{n}(\mathbb{R})$ Def4 : *Degree of Rep* : $\text{dim} V$ is called of Degree of Rep Def5 : *Similar/Isomorphic/Equivalent Reps* : $\rho_1:G \rightarrow \text{GL}(V)$ and $\rho_2: G \rightarrow \text{GL}(V')$ are similar reps if there exists linear isomorphism $\tau : V \rightarrow V'$ s.t $\forall g \in G \,\, \rho_1(g) = \tau \circ \rho_{2}(g) \circ \tau^{-1}$ Def6 : *Trivial Rep*: $\rho(g) = \text{Id} \,\, \forall g$, where $\text{Id}$ is identity transformation on $V$ Def7 : *Regular Rep*: Let $|G| = |V|$ and basis element of $V$ be indexed by group elements $\{e_{g} \}_{g \in G}$, then $\rho(g)(e_{g'}) = e_{gg'}$ is called Regular Rep Def8 : *External Direct Sum of Vector spaces*: Given $V_1,\dots, V_n$ be vector spaces, external direct sum is a new vector space with cartesian product as set and straightforward $V_{1} \oplus_{e} V_{2} \dots \oplus_{e} V_{n} = (V_{1} \times V_{2} \dots \times V_{n}, \mathbf{+}, . )$ with $(v_{1}, \dots, v_{n})\mathbf{+} (u_{1}, \dots, u_{n})$ defined as $(v_{1}+ u_{1}, \dots, v_{n}+u_{n})$ and $a (v_1, \dots, v_n)$ as $(av_1, \dots, av_n)$ Def9 : *External Direct Sum Rep* : Let $\phi^{1},\dots,\phi^{n}$ be reps on $V_1,\dots,V_{n}$ respectively then external direct sum rep denoted as $\phi^{1} \otimes_{e} \phi^{2} \dots \otimes_{e} \phi^{n}: G \rightarrow \text{GL}(V_{1} \oplus_{e} V_{2} \dots \oplus_{e} V_{n})$ sends $(v_{1}, \dots, v_{n}) \mapsto (\phi^{1}(g)(v_1), \phi^{2}(g)(v_2) \dots ,\phi^{n}(g)(v_n))$ Def9 : *Internal Direct Sum*: Let $V_1, \dots, V_{n} \subseteq V$, External direct sum $V_{1} \otimes_{e} V_{2} \dots \otimes_{e} V_{n}$ is internal direct sum if $f : V_{1} \otimes_{e} V_{2} \dots \otimes_{e} V_{n} \rightarrow V$ given by $(v_1,\dots,v_{n}) \mapsto \sum_{i=1}^{n} v_{i}$ is an isomorphism Def10 : *Invariant Subspaces*: $W$ is invariant subspace if $\forall g \in G, \forall w \in W, \rho(g)(w) \in W$ Def11 : *Homorphism of Representation/ Intertwining operator *: $\phi:G \rightarrow \text{GL}(V)$ and $\psi : G \rightarrow \text{GL}(W)$ a morphism of representation between $\phi$ and $\psi$ is a linear map $T : V \rightarrow W$ s.t $\phi(g) \circ T = T \circ \psi(g)$. $\text{Hom}_{G}(\phi, \psi) = \{ T \in \text{Hom}(V,W) | \psi(g) \circ T = T \circ \phi(g) \}$
